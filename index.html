Culture

Brazilian culture, a blend of indigenous, European, African, and immigrant influences, is known for Carnival, samba, football, and a rich tapestry of music, dance, and cuisine, making it a lively and dynamic representation of Brazil's diverse and colorful identity.

Landscape

The Brazilian landscape is remarkably diverse: the Amazon rainforest, the largest in the world, with its incomparable biodiversity, amazing waterfalls like the Iguazu Falls, vast coasts with beautiful beaches and wide savannahs make Brazil a geographically rich and beautiful country.

Football

Brazilian football is a national obsession characterized by skill, passion and a long history. Brazil has produced legendary players like Pelé, Ronaldo, Ronaldinho and Neymar, and the national team has won the FIFA World Cup a record number of times, making soccer an integral part of Brazilian culture.
